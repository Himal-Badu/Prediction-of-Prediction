## Engagement Tactics

### 1. The Thoughtful Reply

**Bad reply:** "Great insight! 🔥"  
**Good reply:** "This mirrors what we found with PoP — meta-attention heads need ~200 samples to calibrate on out-of-distribution tokens, but then generalize well. The latency tradeoff is worth it for production systems."

**Formula:** Acknowledge their point + add your unique perspective or data + leave room for follow-up.

### 2. The Quote Tweet

**How:** Take a tweet from a big account, quote it, and add a genuinely new insight. Don't just agree — extend the idea.

**Example:**
> @bigaccount: "Hallucination detection is still unsolved"
> 
> @poaborisov (quote): "Unsolved in one pass, maybe. We're seeing 83% precision with a meta-learning approach that analyzes attention patterns before generation. The key insight: you don't need to catch every error, just most of them fast."

### 3. The "First Reply" Play

When a big account posts, be one of the first 3 replies. Early replies get seen by thousands. Have notifications on for your top 10 targets.

### 4. The Thread Reply Cascade

When a big account posts a thread, reply to tweet #1 with a summary of your take, then reply to a later tweet with a deeper point. Gets you in multiple reply chains.

### 5. The "You Should Build This" Play

When someone posts about an unsolved problem that PoP solves, reply with: "This is exactly what PoP does — [1 sentence explanation]. Want to see a demo?" Convert frustration into leads.

## Thread Strategy

### Thread Types That Get Engagement

| Type | Engagement | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Technical deep-dive** | 🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥 | "How I trained a meta-learning layer to detect hallucinations in a single pass" |
| **Contrarian take** | 🔥🔥🔥🔥 | "Unpopular opinion: RAG doesn't fix hallucinations — it just adds more tokens to hallucinate with" |
| **Build-in-public** | 🔥🔥🔥🔥 | "Week 8 of building PoP — here's what went wrong and what I learned" |
| **Tutorial/how-to** | 🔥🔥🔥 | "How to evaluate hallucination detection (most people get this wrong)" |
| **Data/visual** | 🔥🔥🔥🔥 | "We benchmarked PoP against 5 methods. Here are the results 📊" |

### Thread Anatomy (Best Performing Structure)

```
Tweet 1: Hook — bold claim or surprising fact. Make people stop scrolling.
Tweet 2: Context — why this matters (market size, pain point, failure stories)
Tweet 3-6: Technical breakdown — show your work. ASCII diagrams, data, code snippets.
Tweet 7: Results — what did you find? Be specific with numbers.
Tweet 8: Implications — what does this mean for the industry?
Tweet 9: CTA — follow for more, link to GitHub, join waitlist, or ask a question.
```

### Thread Timing
- Post threads between **8–10 AM EST** (morning US = global visibility)
- **Tuesday–Thursday** perform best for technical content
- Avoid weekends for important launches (lower engagement)

## DM Strategy

**Goal:** Build genuine relationships, not pitch-slap people.

### Who to DM (After engaging with their posts for 1–2 weeks)
- Fellow builders: "Loved your thread on X. We're solving a similar problem — want to swap notes?"
- Researchers: "Your paper on Y was super relevant to what we're building. Any interest in a quick chat?"
- VCs (later): Only DM after they engage with your content first. Warm intros > cold DMs.

### DM Rules
1. Never lead with a pitch
2. Reference something specific they posted
3. Keep it to 2–3 sentences max
4. Make it easy to say yes ("quick 15-min chat?")
5. Follow up once, then stop. No nagging.

## Anti-Engagement (What NOT to Do)

- ❌ "Great post! Check out my project too" — parasitic engagement
- ❌ Replying to every single post from one person — stalker energy
- ❌ Auto-DMs after follows — instant unfollow
- ❌ Engagement pods — fake engagement, easy to spot
- ❌ Responding to hate with hate — block and move on
- ❌ Subtweeting competitors — focus on your own work
- ❌ Follow-unfollow tactics — burns trust, low ROI anyway
